Fun facts about dogs

01

Dogs have 300 million olfactory receptors in their noses — humans have about 6 million. Their sense of smell is roughly 40× more powerful than ours.

02

A dog's nose print is as unique as a human fingerprint — no two are alike.

03

Dogs dream! Studies show their brain activity during sleep closely mirrors that of humans, suggesting they replay the day's events.

04

The average dog can learn over 165 words, and border collies have been documented learning more than 1,000.

05

Dogs can detect changes in human body chemistry associated with stress, fear, and even certain diseases like diabetes and epilepsy.

06

Puppies are born blind and deaf — their eyes and ears open around 10–14 days after birth.

Benefits of having a dog

💓 Better heart health

Dog owners have lower blood pressure, reduced cholesterol levels, and a significantly lower risk of cardiovascular disease.

🧠 Mental wellbeing

Interacting with dogs boosts oxytocin and serotonin, reducing anxiety and depression. Therapy dogs are used in hospitals and schools worldwide.

🏃 More active lifestyle

Dog owners walk an average of 22 more minutes per day than non-owners — that adds up to over 2.5 hours of extra exercise each week.

👶 Stronger immune systems

Children raised with dogs show lower rates of allergies and asthma. Early exposure to pet dander trains the immune system to be more resilient.

🤗 Social connection

Dogs are social magnets. Walks, dog parks, and training classes create opportunities to meet people and build community.

🛡️ Safety & security

A dog's hearing and smell detect threats long before humans can. Their presence alone deters intruders and provides peace of mind.

A few favourite breeds

Golden Retriever

Famously gentle, patient, and eager to please. The quintessential family dog and one of the most popular breeds worldwide.

Irish Setter

Strikingly beautiful with a flowing red coat and boundless energy. Affectionate, outgoing, and always ready for an adventure.

Labrador Retriever

The world's most popular dog breed for good reason: outgoing, gentle, and adaptable. Outstanding as service and therapy dogs.

Beagle

Curious, merry, and famously nose-driven. Beagles bring a playful spirit to every adventure and get along with everyone they meet.
